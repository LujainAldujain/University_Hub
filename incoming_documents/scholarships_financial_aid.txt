SCHOLARSHIPS AND FINANCIAL AID GUIDE
Office of Financial Aid — Academic Year 2026

1. MERIT-BASED SCHOLARSHIPS
- Presidential Scholarship: full tuition, for entering freshmen with a
  cumulative high-school GPA of 3.9+ and a top-decile admissions test score.
  Renewable each year with a minimum university GPA of 3.5.
- Dean's Scholarship: 50% tuition, for entering freshmen with a GPA of 3.6+.
  Renewable with a minimum university GPA of 3.2.
- Trustee Scholarship: 25% tuition, for entering freshmen with a GPA of 3.3+.
  Renewable with a minimum university GPA of 3.0.

2. NEED-BASED FINANCIAL AID
Students may apply for need-based grants by submitting the annual Financial
Aid Application (FAA) along with proof of household income. Awards are
disbursed each semester directly against the student's tuition account.

3. DEPARTMENTAL SCHOLARSHIPS
Individual academic departments (including Computer Science, Engineering, and
Business) offer competitive scholarships to declared majors in their second
year or later. Applications open each Spring semester through the department
office.

4. ATHLETIC AND ARTS SCHOLARSHIPS
Awarded by the Athletics Department and the College of Fine Arts respectively,
based on tryouts, portfolio review, or audition. These may be combined with
a maximum of one merit-based scholarship, subject to a total aid cap of 100%
of tuition.

5. RENEWAL REQUIREMENTS
All scholarships require full-time enrollment (12+ credit hours per semester)
and satisfactory academic progress. Falling below the minimum GPA for one
semester results in a warning; a second consecutive semester below the
minimum results in scholarship cancellation.

6. EXTERNAL SCHOLARSHIPS
Students receiving external scholarships must report them to the Office of
Financial Aid, as total aid (institutional + external) cannot exceed the
full cost of attendance.
