UNIVERSITY GRADUATION REQUIREMENTS
Office of the Registrar — Academic Year 2026

1. OVERVIEW
To be eligible for graduation and the awarding of a Bachelor's degree, a student
must satisfy all of the following requirements before the end of their final
term of enrollment.

2. CREDIT HOUR REQUIREMENTS
- A minimum of 132 total credit hours must be completed.
- At least 45 of these credit hours must be completed in residence at the
  university (transfer credit cannot exceed 87 hours).
- A minimum cumulative Grade Point Average (GPA) of 2.0 on a 4.0 scale is
  required across all attempted coursework.
- A minimum GPA of 2.0 is also required within the student's declared major.

3. CORE CURRICULUM
Students must complete the University Core Curriculum, consisting of:
- 9 credit hours of Communication (English Composition I & II, Public Speaking)
- 6 credit hours of Mathematics (College Algebra or higher)
- 9 credit hours of Natural Science (including at least one laboratory course)
- 9 credit hours of Humanities and Fine Arts
- 6 credit hours of Social and Behavioral Sciences
- 3 credit hours of Islamic Studies / Ethics

4. MAJOR REQUIREMENTS
Students must complete all required and elective courses specified by their
declared major department, as published in the relevant program handbook.
A senior capstone project or comprehensive exam is required in the final year
for most majors.

5. RESIDENCY REQUIREMENT
The final 30 credit hours of a student's program must be completed at this
university; they may not be transferred in from another institution.

6. APPLICATION FOR GRADUATION
Students must file an Application for Graduation with the Registrar's Office
no later than the start of their final semester. Degree audits are performed
automatically once the application is submitted.

7. ACADEMIC STANDING
Students must not be under active academic suspension or have any unresolved
disciplinary holds at the time of degree conferral.
